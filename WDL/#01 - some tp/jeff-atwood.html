My Works:

I started a programming blog, Coding Horror, in 2004. As a result, I met Joel Spolsky.

Later, on July 17, 2007, as a corollary to Tim Berners-Lee's Rule of Least Power, I proposed Atwood's Law, which states that "Any application that can be written in JavaScript, will eventually be written in JavaScript." (which is now a meme.. which I am sorry for!).

And in 2008, together with Spolsky, we founded Stack Overflow, a programming question-and-answer website. You dont need any explanation for that. But its most used site, even more than Google.. (By developers i mean)! By the way, i am also the first user of StackOverflow... but not the most reputed one... thanks to Jon Skeet.

Then in February 2012, I left Stack Exchange so I could spend more time with my family... And started my new company Civilized Discourse Construction Kit, Inc. (to big a name for company.. I know) which is "open source next-generation discussion platform called Discourse" (thats every awesome word I could put together).
